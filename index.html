Welcome to WSOC 2018

WSOC is an initiative by StreetHack in association with CodeStreet and Mozilla Delhi.

It is an opportunity for students to contribute to open source projects in winters. It is a program which focuses on involving more student developers in the Open Source World.

Projects range in platform and application, including elements of web development, mobile applications, and many others.

Students work for around 15 days on a programming project under the guidance of their Mentor during their university break.

FAQ'S

What is WSOC?

WSOC is an activity that the student performs as an independent developer.

Where does WSOC take place?

Winter Season of Code occurs entirely online; there is no requirement to travel as part of the program.

Who can participate?

Any college or university students are welcome to participate! If you are passionate about programming and are interested in open source contribution

What do I get by completing WSOC?

A certificate and yeah awesome goodies swags and T-Shirts!

Can I participate in WSOC as both a mentor and a student?

No. We want to make sure that each project and student receives sufficient attention, and we feel this could create a bad experience for those involved. Please choose whether participation as a mentor or a student is more appealing to you and plan to apply accordingly.

What programming language(s) should I know to participate in WSOC?

The programming language you need to know depends on which organization you are interested in working with. You should be familiar with the programming language(s) used by that organization.

Can a group submit a proposal together to work on a single project?

No, only an individual may work on a given project.
